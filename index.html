My story

Poured into.
Paying it forward.

I grew up in Pittsburgh knowing I was smart. What I didn't always have was someone who looked like me showing me what was possible. That changed when people started really investing in me.

A Boys & Girls Club that named me Youth of the Year for the entire state of Pennsylvania. Scholarships from the Girlfriends Fund, BGC Shine Bright, and Amazon that saw potential before I had a portfolio. A full ride to Pitt as a Stamps Scholar.

I went back to that same Boys & Girls Club to teach AI and programming to the next generation. I taught young girls cybersecurity at the Sarah Heinz House ROAR event. I mentored incoming Pitt students through SCI UBelong before they ever stepped into their first class. I won the CGI App Dev Competition at 16. Then Amazon — twice. This summer, CGI right here in Pittsburgh.

The gap isn't talent — it's access. It's knowing someone who can open the door. So I built rootEd — the platform I wish existed when I was figuring it all out.
